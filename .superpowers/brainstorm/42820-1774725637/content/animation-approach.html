How should the causal chain animate?

The BFS traces backwards from the anomaly node. How should we visualize each hop?

A

Ripple Wave (Recommended)

The traversal animates hop by hop with a timed delay (300-500ms per hop). Starting node pulses red, then edges glow as they're traversed, then the next node lights up. The wave ripples outward like dropping a stone in water. Each hop adds to the chain — nothing disappears.

t=0ms: ● motor_rpm (anomaly source, pulses red)
t=400ms: ●──━━──● NEMA17 (edge glows orange)
t=800ms: ●──━━──● DRV8825 (next hop, yellow)
t=1200ms: ●──━━──● motor_controller.py (root cause, green)

Color gradient from red (symptom) → orange → yellow → green (root cause). Confidence decays per hop.

B

Spotlight Scan

Instead of a wave, a spotlight moves along the causal path. Only the currently-examined node and its edges are highlighted. Previous hops dim to a faint trail. Feels like a detective scanning through the system.

t=0ms: ● motor_rpm (bright, focused)
t=400ms: ●──━━──● NEMA17 (spotlight moves)
t=800ms: ●──━━──● DRV8825 (previous dims)
t=1200ms: ●──━━──● motor_controller.py (root found!)

Focused attention, but you lose the full chain view until the end.

C

Full Chain Reveal

No per-hop animation. The entire causal chain appears at once after a brief loading state, with the path highlighted as a colored trail from symptom to root cause. Simpler to implement, less dramatic but clearer.

t=0ms: Loading... (spinner)
t=500ms: Full chain revealed:
●──━━──●──━━──●──━━──●
motor_rpm → NEMA17 → DRV8825 → motor_controller.py

Fast and clear, but no "wow" animation moment.
